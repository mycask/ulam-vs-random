Ulam Spiral vs. Random Spiral
=============================
The experiment in this directory demonstrates that the Ulam spiral
indeed contains patterns that cannot be easily explained by the fact
that prime numbers are odd and the odd numbers in Ulam spiral happen to
form diagonal lines.

To demonstrate this, we first plot a 99x99 Ulam spiral. There are 1208
asterisks in this plot that represent the 1208 prime numbers between 1
and 9801, inclusive.

Then we plot a 99x99 random odd numbers' spiral. There are 1208
asterisks in this plot that represent 1208 pesudorandomly chosen odd
numbers between 1 and 9801, inclusive.

The Ulam spiral shows very prominent and long diagonal lines. If the
long diagonal lines of Ulam spiral were merely due to most prime numbers
being odd, then we would expect random odd numbers' spiral to also show
similar long diagonal lines. But the random odd numbers' spiral does not
have such prominent and long diagonal lines.

The next two sections show the output from the
[plotting script](ulam-vs-random.py).
